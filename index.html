Chapter 02
Project Esther
The Heritage Foundation's 44-page blueprint to dismantle the pro-Palestine movement in America. Named after the biblical figure who saved the Jewish people from genocide — but its real purpose is branding critics of Israel as terrorists.

"Branding a broad range of critics of Israel as 'effectively a terrorist support network,' so that they could be deported, defunded, sued, fired, expelled, ostracized and otherwise excluded from what it considered 'open society.'"

The New York Times, May 2025 — describing Project Esther's core objective

Authored By

Victoria Coates, Robert Greenway, and Daniel Flesch — Heritage Foundation operatives who drafted the plan following October 7, 2023. Politico described it as "a lesser-known blueprint from the same creators of Project 2025."

Key Proposals Already Enacted

Reclassification of anti-Zionism as antisemitism in federal policy. University funding threatened for institutions that don't comply. Deportation of legal residents for political speech (the Mahmoud Khalil precedent). Expansion of IHRA antisemitism definition — which includes criticism of Israel — toward federal codification. Defunding of organizations with ties to Palestine solidarity movements.

The Crushing of Student Activists

Mahmoud Khalil, a Columbia University student and legal permanent resident, was detained and ordered deported for attending pro-Palestine protests. DHS told him to "self-deport." Others targeted include Mohsen Mahdawi, Rümeysa Öztürk, and Badar Khan Suri — using the Cold War-era McCarran-Walter Act, weaponized against political speech.
